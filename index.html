Product Design
What is Product Design?
Product Design is making a product to be sold by a company to its customers. Product design is a long process of narrowing down an array of ideas into one idea and then, making that idea a reality.

  • Sealab LLC: design service agency from Austin, TX. Founded in 2014, the employ five staff members experienced in UI, product and graphic design.
2D CAD
What is 2D CAD?
2D CAD, stands for "2-Dimensional Computer-Aided Design" is a design software used to make two-dimensional layouts. 2D CAD has many uses, mainly creating vector-based layouts. One of the most popular 2D CAD software is called AutoCAD.
How is 2D CAD used in tech design?
2D CAD allows engineers to create 2D vector-based 2D drawings, mockups of conceptual designs, within the computer. 2D CAD is essential for engineers, because it saves time, money and resources spent on a project, and it decreases the number of measurement errors.
3D CAD
What is 3D CAD?
3D CAD, like 2D CAD, stands for "3-Dimensional Computer-Aided Design". It is a program used to create 3D-models of objects, such as, chairs, tables, phones, computers, humans, buildings, anything. It is also used to animate said models, like animating two humans fightigs. One of the most popular 3D CAD software used today is called Maya.
How is 3D CAD used in tech design?
3D CAD allows designers to create 3D models that they can animate, or use as a prop for a scene or video-game. Video game studios like Valve, make their own 3D models that they use in their games such as: CS:GO, Half-Life, Portal, etc.

Design Process
What is The Design Process?
The Design Process is a an approach used to break down big projects into smaller chunks to be handled better. The Design Process is used widely by scientists and engineers to break down big projects into steps and identify problems to be tackled and solved. The Design Process is broken down into 6 steps:
Step 1: Define The Problem
This step is very crucial. Find at least one problem to improve on in your design. For example: the annoyance of having to use the stylus to interact with a phone and having a physical keyboard even when you don't need it led to the creation of the first iPhone.
Step 2: Collect Information
Collect sketches, take photographs, collect surveys, or data for inspiration. For example: Steve Jobs looked at the trending phones and gathered all of their perks to include on his iPhone.
Step 3: Brainstorm and Analyze Ideas
Begin sketching out a design based on the data collected in the step 2, make sure the design doesn't have any of the problems outlined in step 1. An example of this step is when Steve Jobs's team needed to figure out what to use instead of the stylus.
Step 4: Develop Solutions
Take sketches and design multiple prototypes to be tested and improved on. For example: Steve Job's team created multiple prototypes of the first iPhone.
Step 5: Gather Feedback
Present the prototypes to as many people as you can to get feedback from them. An example of this is when the iPhone designers presented iPhone prototypes to Apple Executives & Steve Jobs himself.
Step 6: Improve
Look at the feedback gathered of the prototypes and reflect on them. Then, decided on what should be incorporated, it is advised to go back to the step 1 and start over. An example of this step is when Apple iPhone team refined the iPhone based on the feedback gathered by testers.
